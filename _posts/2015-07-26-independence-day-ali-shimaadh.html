Independence Day 2015

written by Ali Shimaadh, Grade 9, Al Madhrasathul Munavvara school

After being a British Colony for about 78 years, Maldives attained independence on July 26, 1965. On the Independence Day, celebrated on July 26 every year, a special official event is held at the Republic Square. The celebrations start in the evening with a march past by the National Security Service and the National Cadet Corps. The march past is followed by performances of drills and traditional dances by local school children.

Maldives Independence Day has a lot of significance, as it is on this day that this country got freedom from the clutches of Britishers. It falls on the 26th in the month of July. In the year 1887, Maldives became a British Protectorate. During that time, the Britishers agreed that they wont interfere in the internal affairs of Maldives. However, when it came to foreign matters, the Britishers had to be consulted.

As a part of agreement, the Britishers said that in return, they would ensure that Maldives is safe and won't be subjected to any colonial intimidation. Later on, on 26th July 1965, Maldivians were granted full independence. Independence Day celebrations in Maldives take place on a large scale. The main highlights of the Independence Day of Maldives consist of the parades by the National Security Services and the National Cadet Corps. It is followed by various performances by school children dressed in colorful costumes.

This is the 50th independence of our country. This day belongs to president Ibrahim Nasir. In this enjoyable day to make more enjoyable our island council and school conduct a parade. In this parade all islanders participated. From elders to youngers took part on this parade. council was the organisers of thi event. Around 200 people took part in this event. This event was started in 5 o'clock in evening.

Ali Shimaadh, Grade 9, Al Madhrasathul Munavvara school
